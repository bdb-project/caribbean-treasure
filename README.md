# Caribbean Treasure

By Garry Francis

## Background

You'd always been fascinated by stories of pirates and sunken galleons and lost treasures. When you learnt how to sail a yacht, you started taking this interest a little more seriously and started studying old maps and manuscripts in various libraries.

Then, one day, you found a musty old manuscript that hinted at the location of a pirate ship that had sunk off the coast of a small uncharted island in the Caribbean. This was what you'd been looking for! After a little more research, you found that the island was uncharted when the manuscript was written, but nothing is uncharted nowadays and you were easily able to find it on current navigation charts.

On your next summer vacation, you rented a small yacht and sailed to the location indicated on the manuscript, where you found a small tropical island far from the normal tourist routes. You moored the yacht in a tranquil bay a few metres offshore and swam to the beach, tingling with excitement. When you explored the area along the beach, it was blocked by a steep cliff, so you retraced your steps.

When you returned to the beach, you noticed footprints in the sand that weren't there before. They weren't yours! They started at the vegetation bordering the beach, headed into the water, then returned and disappeared back into the vegetation. The footprints were only shallow, so the owner must have been very slight of build.

You started to panic. Could the island be inhabited by savages? How many were there? Were they friendly or were they barbaric? Might they have entered the yacht and damaged it. Should you check the yacht or should you follow the footsteps into the vegetation?

This is a traditional text adventure that was written for The BDB Project. The game is hosted at [Caribbean Treasure](https://bdb-project.itch.io/caribbean-treasure).
